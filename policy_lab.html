Higher Education Finance Policy Lab

Choose tuition, grants, loans, repayment, and forgiveness rules. The demo then simulates how a synthetic cohort sorts across college fields, debt exposure, work while enrolled, and occupations. This is a front-end prototype using stylized simulated data, designed to illustrate how the full model-based website could work.

IMPORTANT. This prototype uses simulated data and is provided for example purposes only. The final product may use different data, functionalities, and layouts depending on technical feasibility and the final implementation of the model-based policy laboratory.

Important: This prototype uses simulated data and hand-coded behavioural responses for demonstration only. It is not an empirical estimate. Average-debt figures are shown as contextual benchmarks and should be checked against the linked official or statistical sources; for Denmark, the linked official SU/borger.dk pages document the loan system and repayment, while the graduation-debt benchmark comes from an OECD-reported cross-country comparison; for Germany the figure shown is the BAföG statutory repayment cap, not an average debt-at-graduation measure. Media items in the news sidebar are limited to late 2025 or newer where media coverage is included. In the full project, the website would use the estimated structural model to solve many policy configurations, store their outputs, and interpolate across the policy space.
